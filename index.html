My name is Thomas. If you've come to this page, odds are you're wondering who I am. At least, I hope so, else this'll be really awkward.

About Me

For starts, I was born and raised in Kansas City, MO and have been here my whole life. I work for Rockhurst University as an IT Support Specialist, and love to play video games and watch anime!

Some of you may recognize me from a show called "Queer Eye" on Netflix! (Season 3 Episode 7, you should check it out ;) )

Before you ask, yes it was an amazing experience. Though the time was short, I got a lot out of the experience with each individual interaction with the Fab Five members.

Tan

With Tan, I learned to be a little more adventurous with the types of clothes I wear, and to not be afraid of stripes.

Antoni

With Antoni, I learned I still hate brussel sprouts, but I can use the cooking method to make other yummy vegetables!

Jonathan

With Jonathan, I learned to give my body more attention, and that my appearance isn't just for others, but for me as well.

Karamo

With Karamo, an important lesson I learned is a change in mindset. Being stuck in your head often leads to negative thoughts. Changing the way you view the situation can really change your mood. Instead of being nervous, talk yourself out of the anxiousness, and feel excited. Nervousness and excitement are one and the same, it's just a matter of perspective.

Bobby

Bobby wasn't really a "one-on-one" person, but the work he did on the house was amazing! He helped Karamo out with convincing me to step out of my comfort zone, and walked me through the "get out of your head" talk.

All in all, it was a fantastic experience, and I don't think anything will ever top this for a birthday gift. I'd like to think I could go on and on about the experience, but I really can't. I had a party afterwards at UpDown and that was really awesome. If you haven't been, I strongly encourage you to go if you like Arcades and alcohol. They've got an amazing Macaroni and Cheese pizza that I'm not sure if it was delicious, or drunk Thomas thought it was delicious. Either way, it was delicious.

Thanks!

Thank you for reading my ramble. Again, check out Queer Eye. If not for me, at least to enjoy the show. There are some really awesome people on their, and each episode is inspirational in their own way.
